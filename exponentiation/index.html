One of the simplest method of implementing the tensor of the prime (3) is through the 33's behaviour with the format of 369 where the six (6) is 3+3 and nine (9) is 3x3. So the different is only the operator of plus and times while the variable is remain.

This 33 as number is a composite.

  • The smallest triadic semiprime.
  • The largest integer n such that π(n) = n/3
  • The number of primes and the number of semiprimes up to 33 are equal.
  • The sum of the digits of the first (3+3) primes with a digit-3 equals 33. Note that π(33) = π(3*π(33)).
  • The smallest run of 3 consecutive integers that are semi-primes (the product of two primes) begins with 33.

Let's compare it with the 33th prime number 137 which is appears frequently in the Torah. You may see that the triadic behaviour of 33 is slighly going bilateral by 33th to 34th prime. This fenomena is happened as the major case by the next prime level

  • 137 divides 11111111..
  • 137 is the largest prime factor of 123456787654321.
  • The greatest prime factor of any 8-digit repdigit is 137
  • (137!-1)/139 is prime. Note that 137 and 139 are twin primes.
  • The only known primeval number whose sum of digits equals the number of primes "contained."
