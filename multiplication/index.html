You may learn that sets of algebraic objects has a multilinear relationship related to a vector space called tensor. Tensors may map between different objects such as vectors, scalars, and even other tensors.

Herewith we would like to introduce something similar which is an existence of power of every individual number. This project is actually about mapping the tensors of this power. Let's start with the number two (2) as the first prime of π(10).

By the primes numbers it could be stated that the power of this first prime is still much bigger than the power of the other primes is combined all together. Such of conclution is made since the first presentation ot our mapping system.

Direction:
- The initial of 168 & 329 brings the 102 as 100+2 to π(π(10000))-1=200 or 100 x 2.
- Then the 289 lets this 100x2 to 100² so it brings 100 to 10000 by the power of 2.
- At the last it will be separated by the scheme of 168 to 102 goes back 100 and 2.
 
Conclution:
- All of the other primes than 2 is 1 less than the number n times the number of 2. 
- Those Mersenne primes is generated as 1 less than the power n of the number of 2. 
- Thus they will conseqently be carried out by the same scheme of this number of 2.

Let's observe a living creature such as the objects of each human chromosomes. See that every first of two (2) groups are always identical. It is easily seen that the power this prime is occupying at least 2/3 of the whole objects recursively. This behaviour is what we called as the bilateral way. So it would mean the tensor that happen to an object causing to this prime power.

This is actually the movement of the power of prime 71 to 43 while the form (29,30,31,32) and (33,34,35,36) act as the interface as per Scheme-33 below. See how it makes an affect to the π(10) in relation with the 1's cell that consist of 31+37 or 68. The first of π(10) will work in conjuntion with (Δ1) which equivalent to 19. Same behaviour as the second that brings 19x2 or 38.
